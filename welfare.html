Club Policy

SAFEGUARDING & WELFARE

Merseyside BMX Club is committed to the safety and wellbeing of every young person in our care. Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility.

OUR COMMITMENT

Merseyside BMX Club is a volunteer-led organisation dedicated to promoting safe participation in BMX racing. We follow and adhere to the guidance laid out by British Cycling on all welfare and safeguarding matters and are actively engaged in the welfare and safeguarding of all our member families and riders.

Our Club Welfare Officer has primary responsibility for receiving, managing and reporting concerns about children or adults at risk. Safeguarding is however the responsibility of all members, coaches and volunteers.

All coaches hold valid British Cycling coaching qualifications and are fully DBS checked through British Cycling.

We follow British Cycling's safeguarding framework for affiliated clubs.

WELFARE OFFICER

If you have any concerns regarding the wellbeing or safety of any club member, or the conduct of any rider, parent or carer while representing the club, please contact our Welfare Officer.

CLUB WELFARE OFFICER

Contact us and your message will be directed to the right person.

merseysidebmx@gmail.com

You can also speak to a coach or committee member in person at any club session or event.

REPORTING A CONCERN

British Cycling takes all safeguarding allegations seriously. If you have a concern about a young person or vulnerable adult, please report it. When in doubt, always report.

EMERGENCY OR IMMEDIATE DANGER

Police: 999

NSPCC: 0808 800 5000

Childline: 0800 1111 (for young people, free and confidential)

For concerns that are not an immediate emergency, contact the Club Welfare Officer or speak to British Cycling directly at compliance@britishcycling.org.uk with the subject line "Safeguarding Concerns".
