SECTION 1

ESSENTIAL EQUIPMENT

Starting BMX is a bit different from sports like football or gymnastics where you can walk into any shop and pick up what you need for £100. The kit is more specialist, but you don't need to spend a fortune to get started well.

REQUIRED
FULL FACE HELMET

A BMX or MTB specific full face helmet is mandatory. Don't be tempted by motocross helmets. They are much heavier and less comfortable. Don't buy from toy shops like Smyths or Argos. Buy from a proper bike or BMX supplier.

Mandatory
REQUIRED
GLOVES

Mandatory at all sessions. BMX or MTB gloves are best. They don't need to be expensive. Any full finger glove from a bike supplier will do the job.

Mandatory
REQUIRED
LONG SLEEVES & TROUSERS

Long sleeves and long trousers are required at all sessions. No shorts without knee and shin pads. Race pants are ideal once you're committed, but any suitable sportswear is fine to start.

Mandatory
RECOMMENDED
BODY ARMOUR

Not mandatory but strongly recommended. Upper body armour protects the torso, arms and elbows. Once a rider is committed to the sport this becomes an important investment.

Recommended
RECOMMENDED
KNEE & SHIN PADS

Highly advisable and required if wearing race shorts. Can be worn over race pants. Affordable options are available from most BMX suppliers.

Recommended

SECTION 2

HELMETS

Getting the right helmet is one of the most important purchases you'll make. Fit matters more than price. Always try before you buy where possible, as manufacturer size charts don't always match actual head sizes.

Fitting advice: The helmet should fit snug around the cheeks as well as the top of the head. There should be no gaps and it should not move from side to side or up and down. If possible, buy in person so you can try it on.

SECTION 3

BODY PROTECTION

Two main approaches for upper body protection, depending on budget and how committed your rider is to the sport.

RECOMMENDED ONCE COMMITTED
FULL UPPER BODY ARMOUR

Covers the torso and arms including elbows in one piece. More expensive but offers comprehensive protection. S1 protection is a good option at this level, though it is towards the higher end of the price range.

  • Best overall protection
  • One piece, no fuss
  • Worth the investment once committed
BUDGET FRIENDLY STARTING POINT
ELBOW AND ARM PADS

A more affordable option for riders just getting started. Protects the most common contact points without the full outlay of a complete armour set. More budget friendly options are available but try on for sizing where possible.

  • Lower cost
  • Covers key injury points
  • Good starting option

SECTION 4

UNDERSTANDING BIKE SIZES

BMX race bikes come in a wide range of sizes. The most important measurement is the reach from the seat area to the handlebars. Size depends on the rider's height, inside leg measurement and the frame geometry of the specific brand.

BMX sizing is not the same as mountain bike sizing. It is not always recommended to size up for a rider to grow into, as this can hinder progress. Speak to us if you are unsure.
SECTION 5

COMPLETE BIKE vs CUSTOM BUILD

For new riders the answer is simple: buy a factory complete bike. Custom builds are for experienced riders who have fully committed to the sport.

FOR NEW RIDERS
FACTORY COMPLETE BIKE

The best and most cost effective choice when starting out. Comes fully assembled with frame, wheels, brakes and all components, ready to ride straight away.

  • Best value for beginners
  • Ready to ride immediately
  • All parts matched and compatible
  • Typically £400 to £700 new, £100 to £400 second hand
NOT FOR BEGINNERS
CUSTOM BUILD

Custom builds are for riders who have committed fully to BMX and want a bike designed to their exact specification. They are expensive and unnecessary when starting out.

  • Typically starts at £1,500 upwards
  • Not recommended until fully committed
  • A custom build will not make a rider faster on its own
  • Speak to us before going down this route

SECOND HAND

Second hand bikes can represent great value. The main thing to watch out for is making sure you're buying a race bike, not a park or street BMX. Sizes aren't always clearly described by sellers either, so ask us before committing to a purchase.

Important: When buying online, do not send money upfront for a bike you are not 100% sure about. If in doubt, ask. Our members WhatsApp group is very active and the coaches and committee are always happy to help check a listing before you buy.
