RACE DAY GUIDE

Everything you need to know about race day. How the day flows, the format, Sqorz, the pens and the start gate. Read this before your first race and keep it handy on the day.

GAPS BETWEEN STAGES Motos run back to back with only short gaps. Once knockouts begin there is usually a longer break before quarters and semis, and the gap before finals is typically the longest of the day. Keep checking Sqorz and listening to the PA so you always know where the day is up to.
SECTION 2

PRACTICE SESSIONS

Most race meets include practice time before racing begins. The format varies by event so always check the programme on the day.

TYPICALLY FIRST
OPEN PRACTICE

All riders from all age groups on the track at the same time. Your chance to ride the full course, get comfortable with the surface and warm up. Keep your head up and be aware of other riders around you.

TYPICALLY SECOND
AGE GROUP PRACTICE

Track split into time slots by age group for a less crowded session. Not all events run age group practice. Check the event programme to see when your rider's group is on.

USE PRACTICE WELL Practice is not compulsory but strongly recommended, especially at a track you haven't raced before. A few laps in practice to check the berms and find your line through the rollers makes a big difference when the gate drops.
SECTION 3

HOW A RACE DAY WORKS

All riders start in the Motos and then progress through further rounds depending on how many riders are in their class.

RIDERS IN CLASS
FORMAT
8 or fewer
Grand Prix — 4 motos, no finals. Points winner takes the result.
9 to 16
3 motos, then straight to Finals. Top 8 into the A Final, rest into the B Final.
17 to 32
3 motos, then Semi Finals. Top 16 from motos into Semis. Top 4 from each semi into the A Final, rest into B Final.
33 or more
3 motos, then Quarter Finals (top 32 qualify). Top 4 from each quarter into Semis. Top 4 from each semi into A Final, rest into B Final.
MOTO ROTATION At most UK events, moto rotation is used. You won't race against exactly the same riders in every moto. The draw is rotated to give a fairer reflection of overall ability.
SECTION 4

THE STAGES

Each stage has different stakes. Here is what to expect at each one.

M
MOTOS
ALL RIDERS
MOTOS — QUALIFYING ROUNDS
Every rider does three motos regardless of class or ability. A moto is a single race of up to 8 riders sprinting one lap, lasting roughly 30 to 45 seconds. Points are awarded based on finishing position and added up after all three motos. Lower total is better. Motos run back to back with short gaps throughout the morning.
QF
QUARTERS
33+ RIDERS ONLY
QUARTER FINALS
Only the top 32 from the motos qualify. Top 4 from each heat progress to the Semis. Sudden death — finish 5th or below and your day ends here. Check Sqorz after the motos to see your new race number and gate draw.
SF
SEMIS
17+ RIDERS
SEMI FINALS
Top 16 from the motos qualify. Up to 8 per heat, top 4 go to the A Final. Positions 5 to 8 go to the B Final. Gate positions are seeded on moto results, so riding well earlier gives you a better gate draw here.
F
FINAL
THE BIG ONE
THE FINAL
One race. First across the line wins. No averaging, no points — pure racing. A Final for the top 8, B Final for those who didn't make it. Both count separately for series standings. Race hard all the way to the line.
SECTION 5

THE POINTS SYSTEM

After each moto, riders earn points based on finishing position. These are added up across all three motos. Lower total is better — 1st, 2nd and 2nd scores 5 points, which beats 2nd, 3rd and 4th (9 points).

HOW TIES ARE RESOLVED If two riders finish on equal points after three motos, the tie is resolved by their finishing position in the last moto. Whoever finished higher in the final moto takes the better overall position. If still tied, the moto before that is used, working backwards.
GRAND PRIX FORMAT With 8 or fewer riders, the event runs as a Grand Prix. Riders complete 4 motos and the points winner is the event winner. No separate final race.
SECTION 6

USING SQORZ

Sqorz is the live results system used at most UK BMX races. Race numbers, gate draws, results and standings all live here. No app needed — open a browser and go to our.sqorz.com.

ADD IT TO YOUR HOME SCREEN On iPhone, tap the Share button then "Add to Home Screen". On Android, tap the three dot menu and choose "Add to Home Screen". Much quicker than hunting for it in a browser on race day.
FINDING YOUR RIDER

THE PENS

The pens are the staging area at the back of the start hill where riders wait before heading to the gate. Knowing how to find your pen makes race day much less stressful.

ARRIVE AT THE PENS EARLY Aim to be in the pens at least 10 races before yours. If your race is 46 and race 36 is on the track, it is time to go now. Keep checking Sqorz and listening to the PA.
FOR FIRST TIME RIDERS Tell the marshal it is your first race. They will look after you. Everyone in the pens is there to help.
WRITE IT ON MASKING TAPE Write your race and gate numbers on masking tape and stick it to the back of your race plate before the day starts. No need to check your phone when you are in the pens.
SECTION 8

THE START GATE

Once riders are called to the gate, the automated start sequence begins. Here is exactly what the rider will hear.

OK RIDERS
The sequence has begun. If not ready, shout "Wait!" or "No!" and raise a hand. The sequence restarts. Once past this point the race is in progress.
RIDERS READY
Fully set and in position. The start is now in progress and will not stop. Focus on the lights.
WATCH THE LIGHTS
The traffic light sequence begins. Somewhere between 0 and 2.5 seconds the lights turn green and the gate drops. The race is on.
GATE DROPS
Sprint hard all the way to the line. Many races are decided in the final few pedals. After crossing, brake carefully and watch for riders behind.
IT IS YOUR RESPONSIBILITY No one will come to find you. Keep checking Sqorz and listening to the PA. It is the rider's job to be at the gate on time.
SECTION 9

RACE DAY CHECKLIST

Screenshot this or print it off. Nothing worse than arriving and realising you have forgotten something.

BEFORE YOU ARRIVE
Entry confirmed, signed in on Sqorz (green tick next to name)
Race plate and side plates correctly fitted
Transponder attached to front fork (if using)
Full race kit ready: helmet, long sleeves, trousers, gloves
Phone charged, Sqorz bookmarked or on home screen
Masking tape and pen for writing race numbers
WHEN YOU ARRIVE
Go to race registration to confirm entry
Open Sqorz and find your event and class
Note race numbers and gate draws
Write numbers on masking tape on your plate
Find the pens and check the track layout
DURING RACING
Head to pens at least 10 races before yours
Tell the pen marshal your name and race number
Check Sqorz after each race for results and next race info
Hydrate and recover between motos
After motos: confirm if you have progressed and find new race number
DON'T LEAVE EARLY
Stay until all finals have run
Confirm on Sqorz you haven't qualified before leaving
Every position counts for series standings, even in a B Final
SECTION 10

GLOSSARY

Quick reference for the terms you will hear on race day.

Moto
A single qualifying race heat. Every rider does 3 motos to accumulate points.
Grand Prix
Format for 8 or fewer riders. 4 motos, points decide the winner. No separate final.
Quarter Final
Run with 33 or more riders. Top 32 from motos qualify. Top 4 per heat advance.
Semi Final
Run with 17 or more riders. Top 4 advance to A Final. Positions 5 to 8 go to B Final.
A Final
The main final. Top 8 riders. One race to decide the winner.
B Final
Secondary final for riders who didn't make the A Final. Counts for series standings.
Gate
Your numbered starting lane. 8 lanes marked 1 to 8.
Pen
The staging area where riders wait before heading to the start gate.
Race Number
Your assigned number for each heat. The last digit is your pen number.
Gate Number
The number after the colon in Sqorz. For example 46:3 means gate 3.
Holeshot
Being first through the first corner after the gate drops. A big advantage.
Transponder
A small timing chip (MYLAPS) on the bike's front fork that records your times.
Sqorz
The digital event management system used at most UK BMX races. our.sqorz.com.
Berm
A banked corner on the track.
DNS
Did Not Start. Usually receives points equal to the number of riders in the race plus 2.
Moto Rotation
A system that rotates which riders race together in each moto for a fairer result overall.
