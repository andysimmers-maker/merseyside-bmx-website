Development

COACHING PATHWAY

Every rider is different. Our pathway is designed to support everyone — whether you're just finding your feet on the track or chasing a national title. Progress is about development, not just speed.

VIEWING AS
🏁

DEVELOPMENT FIRST

We focus on long-term rider development, not short-term results. Every rider progresses at their own pace — what matters is improvement, effort and enjoyment.

🚲

RACING ISN'T EVERYTHING

Not every rider needs to race to progress and succeed. We have a clear non-racing pathway for riders who want to develop skills and confidence without the pressure of competition.

⭐

MORE THAN SPEED

Attitude, effort and coachability matter just as much as lap times. Riders who work hard, listen well and support their teammates will always progress here.

The Levels

THE PATHWAY

Four levels, each with a clear purpose. Riders move through them based on skill, race readiness and attitude — not age or how long they've been at the club.

LEVEL 2
CLUB DEVELOPMENT
Building foundations and confidence

WHO THIS IS FOR

New and developing riders finding their feet. Club racers, first-time or occasional racers, and riders who aren't yet ready for regional competition.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Gate start basics
  • Cornering, pumping and pedalling
  • Track awareness and riding safely with others
  • Building confidence across the full track

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Consistent participation, growing confidence, clear skill improvement, and competing in at least one club race series.

LEVEL 3
REGIONAL PREPARATION
Getting race-ready at regional level

WHO THIS IS FOR

Strong club racers confident at Expert level who are ready to take on regional competition, or early-stage regional riders building their race experience.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Race craft and positioning
  • Riding under pressure with other riders
  • Consistency at race speed
  • Tactical awareness on the track

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Consistent race execution at regional level, competing in at least 5 regional events (Novice or Expert).

LEVEL 4
NATIONAL PATHWAY
Competing and developing at the highest level

WHO THIS IS FOR

Riders consistently performing at regional level with the potential or ambition to compete nationally. This level also includes development riders who show clear potential even if they haven't yet hit all performance benchmarks.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Advanced technical execution under pressure
  • Gate starts and first straight dominance
  • Race tactics and in-race decision making
  • Physical conditioning and mental preparation

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Competing at national level with consistency. Progress measured by improvement as much as by results — development riders are supported with individual focus areas.

NON-RACING PATHWAY
DEVELOPMENT PATHWAY
Skills, confidence and enjoyment — no racing required

WHO THIS IS FOR

Riders who aren't ready to race yet, or who simply choose not to. This is a fully supported pathway — not a waiting room. Riders here are progressing, developing and an important part of the club.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Skill development at your own pace
  • Building confidence on the track
  • Enjoyment and engagement with the sport
  • Transitioning to Level 2 when you're ready

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Growth in skills and confidence. There's no pressure to race — riders move into the racing pathway when they feel ready, completely at their own pace.

Moving Up

HOW YOU PROGRESS

Progression isn't automatic. Coaches assess riders across several areas before recommending a move to the next level. It's about being ready — not just being fast.

LEVEL 2 → LEVEL 3
✓
SKILL COMPETENCY Confident on the full track. Controlled cornering and pedalling. Safe and consistent riding with a group.
✓
RACE READINESS Can complete a full lap under pressure. Comfortable riding alongside multiple riders. Basic gate start execution.
✓
COACH ASSESSMENT Positive attitude and effort. Receptive to coaching. Consistent performance across sessions.
✓
KIT REQUIREMENTS Own race BMX in good condition. Full-face helmet and body armour. Race jersey, pants or shorts. Transponder for timing.
LEVEL 3 → LEVEL 4
✓
SKILL COMPETENCY Maintain speed through corners. Manual through rollers consistently. Jump confidently under pressure. Controlled, effective gate starts.
✓
RACE PERFORMANCE Consistent top-half finishes. Strong first straight positioning. Ability to ride effectively in a pack.
✓
COACH ASSESSMENT Attitude, effort and coachability. Consistent across sessions. Readiness for higher intensity coaching and expectations.
✓
PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS Assessed against sprint, start, lap time and skill benchmarks. Minimum 4 of 6 benchmarks required — improvement is tracked alongside absolute scores.
Assessment

HOW WE ASSESS RIDERS

Every rider is reviewed every 6 weeks. We look at four equal areas — it's not just about how fast you are. A rider who works hard, listens and supports others will always progress.

25%
PHYSICAL
Sprint speed and fitness
25%
TECHNICAL
Manuals, jumps and cornering
25%
TACTICAL
Starts, first straight position, race decisions
25%
BEHAVIOUR & MINDSET
Effort, attitude, listening and team behaviour
Important: We track improvement as well as raw scores. A rider who improves consistently will always be recognised, even if they're not the fastest in the group.

THE 6-WEEK CYCLE

WKS 1–4
Development — skill drills, race simulations and strength work appropriate to each level.
WEEK 5
Competitive simulation — full gate racing, timed motos and head-to-head racing.
WEEK 6
Testing week — sprint times, start times, lap times and skill tests. Results feed into the rider review.
Questions about your rider's progress? Speak to their coach after a session, or get in touch and we'll be happy to talk it through.
