Development

COACHING PATHWAY

Every rider is different. Our pathway supports everyone — whether your rider is just finding their feet or chasing a national title. Progress is about development, not just speed.

VIEWING AS
🏁

DEVELOPMENT FIRST

We focus on rider development for the long term, not short term results. Riders progress at their own pace — what matters is improvement, effort and enjoyment.

🚲

RACING ISN'T EVERYTHING

Not every rider needs to race to progress. We have a clear non-racing pathway for riders who want to develop skills and confidence without the pressure of competition.

⭐

MORE THAN SPEED

Attitude, effort and coachability matter just as much as lap times. Riders who work hard, listen well and support their teammates will always progress here.

The Levels

THE PATHWAY

Four levels, each with a clear purpose. Riders move through them based on skill, race readiness and attitude, not age or how long they have been at the club.

LEVEL 2
CLUB DEVELOPMENT
Building foundations and confidence

WHO THIS IS FOR

New and developing riders finding their feet. Club racers, occasional racers, and riders not yet ready for regional competition.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Gate start basics
  • Cornering, pumping and pedalling
  • Track awareness and riding safely with others
  • Building confidence across the full track

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Consistent participation, growing confidence, clear skill improvement, and competing in at least one club race series.

LEVEL 3
REGIONAL PREPARATION
Getting race-ready at regional level

WHO THIS IS FOR

Strong club racers confident at Expert level who are ready for regional competition, or early-stage regional riders building their race experience.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Race craft and positioning
  • Riding under pressure with other riders
  • Consistency at race speed
  • Tactical awareness on the track

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Consistent race execution at regional level, competing in at least 5 regional events (Novice or Expert).

LEVEL 4
NATIONAL PATHWAY
Competing and developing at the highest level

WHO THIS IS FOR

Riders consistently performing at regional level with the ambition to compete nationally. Includes development riders who show clear potential even if they haven't yet hit all benchmarks.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Advanced technical execution under pressure
  • Gate starts and first straight dominance
  • Race tactics and in-race decision making
  • Physical conditioning and mental preparation

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Competing at national level with consistency. Progress is measured by improvement as much as results. Development riders are supported with individual focus areas.

NON-RACING PATHWAY
DEVELOPMENT PATHWAY
Skills, confidence and enjoyment. No racing required.

WHO THIS IS FOR

Riders who aren't ready to race yet, or who simply choose not to. This is a fully supported pathway — not a waiting room. Riders here are progressing just as much as anyone else.

WHAT YOU'LL WORK ON

  • Skill development at your own pace
  • Building confidence on the track
  • Enjoyment and engagement with the sport
  • Transitioning to Level 2 when ready

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

Growth in skills and confidence. No pressure to race. Riders move into the racing pathway when they feel ready.

Moving Up

HOW YOU PROGRESS

Progression is not automatic. Coaches assess riders across several areas before recommending a move to the next level. It is about being ready, not just being fast.

Assessment

HOW WE ASSESS RIDERS

Every rider is reviewed every 6 weeks across four equal areas. It's not just about how fast riders are — attitude and effort count just as much as speed.

25%
PHYSICAL
Sprint speed and fitness
25%
TECHNICAL
Manuals, jumps and cornering
25%
TACTICAL
Starts, first straight position, race decisions
25%
BEHAVIOUR & MINDSET
Effort, attitude, listening and team behaviour
Important: We track improvement as well as raw scores. A rider who improves consistently will always be recognised, even if they're not the fastest in the group.

THE 6-WEEK CYCLE

WKS 1–4
Development — skill drills, race simulations and conditioning appropriate to each level.
WEEK 5
Competitive simulation — full gate racing, timed motos and head-to-head racing.
WEEK 6
Testing week — sprint times, start times, lap times and skill tests. Results feed into the rider review.
Questions about a rider's progress? Speak to their coach after a session, or get in touch and we'll be happy to talk it through.
